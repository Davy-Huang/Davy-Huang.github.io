Computer Vision

Background:
   Senior year of college, I worked with a team to design a door opening mechanism for the handicap. The system was designed to make daily life easier for those confined to a wheelchair. Utilizing Machine Learning, I created a Computer Vision module trained to identify doorknobs. A kinect's camera would then be utilized to locate the doorknob and a robotic arm would unlatch it. Shown above is my contribution to project as of December 2016.

Project Details:
   A Haar-like Object Cascade Classifier was trained to identify a specific type of door handle (a door lever) and demonstrate detection by drawing a circle around the detected object. To view the Classifier for door knobs or the progress of the project go to my Github.

Cell Representations

Background:
   As a research assistant at the Boston University Medical Campus studying Glaucoma, I helped construct 3D models of cells from 2D sectional images. Red indicates the cell itself. The blue and green are giant vacuoles within the cell. Tiny pores on the surface of the cell are associated with the drainage of aqueous fluid (a factor in Glaucoma). As shown above, however, these pores are very small in comparison to the long endothelium cells and thus difficulty to study in their 2D format.

Project Details:
   Utilizing sectional imaging and applying engineering CAD tools to biological data, the entire cell can be reconstructed in 3D and studied as a whole. The video below outline the process.

Monitor design

   This vertical monitor was constructed by combining a recycled lcd screen, a controller board, and a corkboard. It is wall mounted to increase space utilization and is designed to be compatible with HDMI and VGA standards. There is additional space for a Raspberry Pi add-on.
