Computer Vision

Background:
   I am working in a team to design a door opening mechanism for the handicap. The system will make daily life easier for those confined to a wheelchair. Utilizing Machine Learning, a Computer Vision module trained to identify doorknob will detect the location of door handles and open them via a robotic arm. Shown above is my contribution to project as of December 2016.

Project Details:
   A Haar-like Object Cascade Classifier was trained to identify a specific type of door handle (a door lever) and demonstrate detection by drawing a circle around the detected object. To view the Classifier for door knobs or the progress of the project go to my Github.

Cell Representations

Background:
   As a research assistant at the Boston University Medical Campus studying Glaucoma, I work with 3D models of cells. Red indicates the cell itself. The blue and green are giant vacuoles within the cell. Tiny pores on the surface of the cell are associated with the drainage of aqueous fluid (a factor in Glaucoma). As shown above, however, these pores are very small in comparison to the long endothelium cells and thus difficulty to study.

Project Details:
   Utilizing sectional imaging and applying engineering CAD tools to biological data, the entire cell can be reconstructed in 3D and studied as a whole. The video below outline the process.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicle-Skyhunter

Background:
   As part of Boston University's Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) team, the Skyhunter fixed wing was utilized as a frame for a package pickup and delivery project.

Project Details:
   Modeled in Solidworks, this design would allow center of mass, airflow, and other features to be quickly recalculated as components are added and removed.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicle-Bent Arm UAV

Background:
   This model was designed as part of BU's UAV team.

Project Details:
   The 3D printed arms were evaluated to provide cushioning space between the motors/propellers and the ground during landings. Modular bars (attached in the image) were design to buffer the propellers against wall collision during flight.
